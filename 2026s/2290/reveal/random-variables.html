General binomial RV $X$:

$X=$ # of heads in $n$ flips of a coin with probability $\begin{cases}p&\text{heads}\\q&\text{tails}\end{cases}$

$\Rightarrow\boxed{P(X=k)=\binom{n}{k}p^{k}q^{n-k}}$

Example 2. Prob. of exactly 2 answers correct on 10 ABCD questions:

$\Rightarrow n=10,k=2,p=0.25,q=0.75$

$\Rightarrow P(X=2)=\binom{10}{\,2}(0.25)^{2}(0.75)^{8}$

General geometric RV $Y\sim \text{Geometric}(p)$:

$Y=$ # of T's before an H flipping a coin w. probability $\begin{cases}p&\text{heads}\\q&\text{tails}\end{cases}$

$\Rightarrow\boxed{P(Y=k)=q^{k}p}$

Example 3. Prob. a fair dice is rolled 4 times before a 5th roll is a 1.

$\Rightarrow P(Y=4)=(5/6)^{4}\cdot (1/6)$

Binomial and geometric RVs are discrete RVs.

But there are also continuous RVs.

Probability density function (PDF) of $X$
$P(X=x)$

Domino's Pizza is done at a time $X$ that varies uniformly between 15 and 25 min.

PDF of $X$ is constant with total area 1, so $f(x)=p(X=x)=1/(25-15)=0.1$ if $15\le x\le 25$ and $f(x)=0$ otherwise.

In general:

PDF $f_X(x)$ of a continuous RV $X$ is a non-negative function with total area 1.

CDF $F_X(x)$ of a continuous RV $X$ increases from 0 to 1.

Uniform RV $X\sim U(a,b)$ between $a$ and $b$ has PDF $f_X(x)=\dfrac{\;\;\;1}{b-a}$ if $a\le x\le b$ and $f_X(x)=0$ otherwise.

Example 4. An RV $X$ has PDF $f(x)=cx^{-1/2}\text{ for }0\le x\le 1$ and $f(x)=0\text{ otherwise}$.

(a) Find $c$. (b) Find $F(x)$. (c) Find median of $X$.

(a) $1=\int_{0}^{1}cx^{-1/2}dx=[2cx^{1/2}]^{1}_{0}=2c\Rightarrow c=\dfrac{1}{2}.$

(b) For $x\ge 0$, $F(x)=\int_{0}^{x}(1/2)t^{-1/2}dt=[t^{1/2}]^{x}_{0}=\sqrt{x}$

$\Rightarrow F(x)=\begin{cases}0&\text{if }x<0\\\sqrt{x}&\text{if }0\le x\le1\\1&\text{if }x>1\\\end{cases}$

(c) $\dfrac{1}{2}=F(x)=\sqrt{x}\Rightarrow x=\dfrac14.$
